Rowing and team work

Lessons learned from rowing about teamwork, synchronization, and harmony in software development teams.

Team LeadershipSoftware DevelopmentAgile

Istanbul is definitely a chaotic city and sometimes it might be really hard to live, but there are still many great opportunities to enjoy life. One of them is rowing. I have been rowing for more than 10 years and I can say that it is one of the best sports that I have ever done. It is not just a sport, it is a way of life. It teaches you many things about life, teamwork, and yourself.

In rowing, the most important thing is harmony. When you are in a boat with other people, you have to be in perfect sync with them. If one person is not in sync, the whole boat will be affected. This is exactly the same in software development teams. When we are working on a project, we have to be in sync with our team members. If one person is not in sync, the whole project will be affected.

In a rowing boat, you have different roles:

  • The coxswain who steers and coordinates
  • The stroke who sets the rhythm
  • The rowers who follow the stroke

This is similar to a software development team where we have:

  • Project managers who coordinate
  • Tech leads who set the technical direction
  • Developers who implement the solutions

In rowing, synchronization is crucial. When you are rowing, you have to:

  1. Start at the same time
  2. Move at the same speed
  3. Apply the same power
  4. Follow the same rhythm

This is exactly what we need in software development:

  1. Start sprints together
  2. Move at a sustainable pace
  3. Apply consistent effort
  4. Follow the same development practices

The key lessons from rowing that apply to software development teams:

  1. Communication is Key

    • In rowing, the coxswain constantly communicates with the team
    • In software development, clear communication is essential for success

  2. Trust Your Team

    • In rowing, you have to trust that everyone will do their part
    • In software development, trust is the foundation of effective teamwork

  3. Practice Makes Perfect

    • In rowing, teams practice together to achieve perfect synchronization
    • In software development, regular team practices (like code reviews) improve quality

  4. Focus on the Goal

    • In rowing, everyone focuses on reaching the finish line together
    • In software development, keeping the end goal in mind helps maintain alignment
